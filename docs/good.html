Good foods

Foods recommended during a disaccharide-free elimination diet (Step 1). Individual tolerance varies — reintroduce and test before assuming long-term safety.

Fats

  • Canola oil
  • Corn oil
  • Flaxseed oil
  • Lard
  • Margarine or diet spread (no whey or milk solids)
  • Meat drippings
  • Olive oil
  • Safflower oil
  • Sunflower oil

Milk and milk products

  • Greek yoghurt (unsweetened)
  • Hard cheese (e.g. cheddar)
  • Unsweetened lactose-free milk
  • Unsweetened nut milk
  • Unsweetened soy milk

Fruits

  • Blackberry
  • Blueberry
  • Cherry
  • Cranberry
  • Currant
  • Damson plum
  • Fig
  • Gooseberry
  • Grape
  • Guava
  • Kiwi fruit
  • Lemon
  • Lime
  • Loganberry
  • Passion fruit

Vegetables

  • Asian greens (e.g. bok choy)
  • Avocado
  • Bell pepper (green and red)
  • Broccoli
  • Brussels sprouts
  • Cabbage
  • Cauliflower
  • Celery
  • Chives
  • Cucumber
  • Eggplant
  • Garlic
  • Green beans
  • Green onion
  • Kale
  • Lettuce
  • Mushroom
  • Parsley
  • Parsnip
  • Radish
  • Spinach
  • Swiss chard
  • Tomato
  • Turnip
  • Watercress
  • Zucchini

Grains

  • Konjac flour
  • Shirataki noodles

Meat and meat substitutes

  • Beef
  • Chicken
  • Duck
  • Egg (plain)
  • Fish
  • Lamb
  • Pork
  • Pork rind
  • Shellfish
  • Tempeh
  • Tofu
  • Turkey
  • Wild game

Sugars and sweeteners

  • Aspartame
  • Cyclamate
  • Dextrose
  • Fructose
  • Glucose
  • Honey
  • Levulose
  • Saccharine

Other

  • Flavour enhancer 621 (MSG)
  • Gluten-free soy sauce
  • Mustard (wholegrain, Dijon)
  • Sea salt
  • Spices and herbs

Baseline: Ideal Nutrition — CSID management guide. Tiered tolerance: CSID Cares — Choosing Your Foods.

Individual tolerance varies. Reintroduce foods one at a time after 2–4 weeks on elimination.
