Bad foods

Foods to avoid during a disaccharide-free elimination diet (Step 1). Many adults can tolerate some of these after structured reintroduction testing.

Fats

  • Butter
  • Margarine with whey or milk solids

Milk and milk products

  • Flavoured yoghurt
  • Milk and milk products (general)

Fruits

  • Apple
  • Apricot
  • Banana
  • Date
  • Grapefruit
  • Mango
  • Melon
  • Papaya
  • Peach
  • Pear
  • Pineapple
  • Raspberry
  • Strawberry

Vegetables

  • Beetroot
  • Brown onion
  • Carrot
  • Corn
  • Green peas
  • Legumes (black beans, chickpeas, lentils, etc.)
  • Okra
  • Potato (all types)
  • Pumpkin
  • Squash (winter and starchy types)

Grains

  • Amaranth
  • Barley
  • Buckwheat
  • Bulgur
  • Corn (grain/flour)
  • Couscous
  • Grain flours (general)
  • Millet
  • Oats
  • Quinoa
  • Rice
  • Rolled oats
  • Rye
  • Spelt
  • Triticale
  • Wheat
  • White rice

Meat and meat substitutes

  • Breaded meat
  • Canned corned beef
  • Cured meat
  • Edamame
  • Egg with added milk, flour, or sugar
  • Processed meat
  • Smoked meat

Nuts and seeds

  • Almond
  • Brazil nut
  • Cashew
  • Hazelnut
  • Macadamia
  • Pecan
  • Pistachio
  • Pumpkin seeds
  • Sesame seeds
  • Sunflower seeds
  • Walnut

Sugars and sweeteners

  • Foods with added sugars
  • Lactose (milk sugar)
  • Maltose (grain sugar)
  • Sucrose (table sugar)
  • Syrups

Baseline: Ideal Nutrition — CSID management guide. Tiered tolerance: CSID Cares — Choosing Your Foods.

Individual tolerance varies. Reintroduce foods one at a time after 2–4 weeks on elimination.
